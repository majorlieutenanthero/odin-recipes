Description

One of the favorite dish native to the Philippines, adobo refers to the method of marinating meat, seafood, or vegetables in a combination of soy sauce and vinegar.

The use of vinegar is no foreign concept to us Filipinos when cooking. Our ancestors used to cook foods or seafood in vinegar to preserve their freshness.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken
  • 3 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 8 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 4 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 1 1/2 cups of water
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon whole peppercorn
