Description

One of the favorite dish native to the Philippines, adobo refers to the method of marinating meat, seafood, or vegetables in a combination of soy sauce and vinegar.

The use of vinegar is no foreign concept to us Filipinos when cooking. Our ancestors used to cook foods or seafood in vinegar to preserve their freshness.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken
  • 3 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 8 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 4 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 1 1/2 cups of water
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon whole peppercorn

Steps

  1. Combine chicken, soy sauce, and garlic in a large bowl. Mix well. Marinate the chicken for at least 1 hour. NOTE: the longer the time you marinate the chicken, the better. 2 lbs chicken, 8 tablespoon soy sauce.

  2. Heat a cooking pot. Pour cooking oil. 3 tablespoons cooking oil.

  3. When the oil is hot enough, pan-fry the marinated chicken for 2 minutes per side.

  4. Pour-in the remaining marinade, including garlic. Add water. Bring to a boil. 1 1/2 cups water

  5. Add dried bay leaves and whole peppercorn. Simmer for 30 minutes or until the chicken gets tender. 3 pieces dried bay leaves, 1 teaspoon whole peppercorn

  6. Add vinegar. Stir and cook for 10 minutes. 4 tablespoons white vinegar

  7. Put-in the sugar, and salt. Stir and turn the heat off. Serve hot. Share and Enjoy! 1 teaspoon sugar, 1/4 teaspoon salt

Notes

  1. Chicken: slice the chicken into serving pieces before cooking. The cuts are usually smaller. For example, a regular piece of chicken breast can be divided into 2 to 3 pieces depending on your preference.

  2. Bay leaves: This is an essential ingredient for Filipino adobo. Dried bay leaves which is locally called "Dahon ng Laurel" are usually used becaused of its availabalibity year round. It can also be stored longer in room temperature. However, fresh bay leaves can also be utilized for this recipe.

  3. Soy sauce: It is preferred to use Filipino brand soy sauce in making adobo as the dish will taste more authentic if local soy sauce is used. Note: It is still possible to use foreign brand of soy sauce if a Filipino brand soy sauce is not available.

  4. Vinegar: White vinegar is the most common type to use for adobo to prolong the lifespan of the dish once cooked and add more Filipino taste. Cane or rice vinegar can also be an alternative ingredient.

  5. Garlic: Adobo should be garlicy. The garlic can be sauteed and boiled just like in this recipe, or it can be toasted. Toast by browning the crushed garlic in oil before adding the chicken. It is also possible to have an extra toasted garlic to have more for garnish later on.

  6. Sugar: This is an optional ingredient. Sugar is added to balance the saltiness of the dish. It is possible to add more sugar if the chicken adobo is needed to be sweeten.

  7. Salt: Also optional, as soy sauce is salty to begin with. Only add salt if needed.

  8. Peppercorn: It does not matter if whole, crushed, or grounded. Whole peppercorn has just been used traditionally as it adds flavor to every dished around the world.
